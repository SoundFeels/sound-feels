Listen & Learn

What is sound? Sound is the audible vibrations that travel through the air. Did you know that there are different colors of sound? Yep, sound has color! This has to do with the different frequencies.

A frequency is the number of cycles that a sound wave vibrates per second. Amplitude is the volume or how loud a sound sounds.

During this activity page, you will be able to listen and read a little bit about each of the different colors of sound! In the game section on the Make Some Noise page, you will also be able to experience playing with amplitudes!

White Noise

What does White Noise remind you of, what do you hear? Did you say it sounded like rain, wind or a fireplace? These are the sounds that people can sometimes hear and are reminded of when they listen to White Noise. White Noise can help people sleep.

Pink Noise

What does Pink Noise sound like to you? Pink noise should sound flat an even, with very little changes. People use Pink noise to test the speakers of a sound system. Pink Noise can improve creativity and concentration.

Note: Before selecting another color of noise on the left, pause the current sound.

⊗

Brown Noise

Does Brown Noise sound familiar to you? Brown Noise is similar to white noise, except is has a lower frequency. People say that Brown Noise reminds them of a waterfall.

Note: Before selecting another color of noise on the left, pause the current sound.

⊗

Blue Noise

Blue Noise is a higher frequency sound that increases while it cycles through the sound. Blue noise sounds like a hissing noise representative of a snake. There is no base in this color of sound.

Note: Before selecting another color of noise on the left, pause the current sound.

⊗

Violet Noise

Violet Noise is the opposite of Brown Noise. Violet noise has a high frequency. To some people Violet Noise can sound like an open water faucet.

Note: Before selecting another color of noise on the left, pause the current sound.

⊗

Grey Noise

Grey Noise has a more powerful start and ending in the frequency. This noise can be used to test your hearing. It has a nice balance to the human ear. Grey Noise can sound to be three-dimensional.

Note: Before selecting another color of noise on the left, pause the current sound.
